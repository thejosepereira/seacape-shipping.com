Our Shipping Routes

Four primary trade corridors connecting the world's key loading and discharging ports, supported by Seacape's network across the Americas, Europe, and Asia.

01
Corridor 1

Black Sea & Mediterranean

One of the world's most active bulk shipping corridors, the Black Sea and Mediterranean route connects major grain-producing nations in Eastern Europe with industrial consumers across Western Europe, North Africa, and beyond. Seacape has operated in this region for decades, building deep expertise in port operations, charterers, and local agents.

Our coverage includes the major Ukrainian, Romanian, and Bulgarian loading ports, as well as principal discharging destinations in Turkey, Egypt, Italy, Spain, and further west.

02
Corridor 2

East Coast & Gulf

With our headquarters in Miami, Florida, Seacape has unparalleled access and knowledge of US East Coast and Gulf ports. This corridor is critical for US agricultural exports, steel imports, and the movement of bulk commodities between North America and global markets.

Our Miami office provides real-time coordination and hands-on support for all shipments moving through this corridor, ensuring regulatory compliance, port efficiency, and seamless cargo handling.

03
Corridor 3

Far East — Japan & China

Asia is the world's largest consumer of dry bulk commodities. Seacape's Far East corridor connects steel producers, coal importers, and agricultural buyers in Japan, China, South Korea, and Southeast Asia with supply origins across the Americas and Europe.

Our established relationships with Asian charterers, port agents, and shipowners allow us to execute efficiently in this highly competitive market, providing our clients with reliable coverage and competitive rates.

04
Corridor 4

West & Central South America

South America represents one of the most dynamic trade corridors for Seacape. With direct office representation through our Santiago, Chile operation and regional presence in Peru and Brazil, we are uniquely positioned to serve this market from both ends.

From copper concentrate and fertilizers on the Pacific coast to soybeans, iron ore, and corn on the Atlantic side, our South American coverage is comprehensive and deeply experienced.

05
Corridor 5

North Coast & South America

Seacape maintains an active presence along the North Coast of South America, covering key Caribbean basin and Atlantic-facing ports across Colombia, Venezuela, and surrounding nations. This corridor supports the movement of coal, fertilizers, steel, and bulk commodities to and from regional markets.

Combined with our established South American network, this corridor connects Caribbean and northern ports with global origins and destinations, providing comprehensive coverage of the entire South American continent.
